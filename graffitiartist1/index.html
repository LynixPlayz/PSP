Business Owners & Government Leaders: Some individuals in positions of authority might view graffiti as a nuisance and vandalism, as it is often placed on private property without permission. They may support measures like using special paints that make graffiti easy to remove or coordinating graffiti removal teams.

Graffiti Artists: Graffiti artists may argue that their work should be recognized as legitimate art. They might feel insulted when their creations are painted over, emphasizing the time and effort invested in their art. Some may advocate for designated spaces where they can showcase their skills without facing legal consequences.

Local Residents: Residents may have mixed opinions, with some suggesting that graffiti artists redirect their talents towards more acceptable forms of art. They may view graffiti as a potential artistic expression but also acknowledge the concerns of property owners who see it as an annoyance.

Legal Graffiti Spaces vs. Art Training Programs

Your choice reflects your commitment to either strongly advocate for art training programs, emphasizing the importance of preserving artistic freedom, or support legal graffiti spaces with a cultural emphasis, seeking a balance with local authorities.
